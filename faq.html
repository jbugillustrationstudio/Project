Transthoracic echocardiogram (TTE) is a non-invasive ultrasound test that provides detailed images of the heart's structure and function. (45 mins)
Electrocardiogram (ECG): This test records the electrical activity of the heart, helping to identify any irregularities in heart rhythm. (5 mins)
Holter monitor is a portable ECG device worn by the patient for 24 to 48 hours to continuously monitor heart rhythm and detect any abnormalities.
Blood tests are conducted to measure cholesterol levels, including total cholesterol, LDL (bad cholesterol), HDL (good cholesterol) and triglycerides, which are essential for assessing cardiovascular health. (?Fasting required)
These examinations are crucial for diagnosing and managing cholesterol-related conditions, allowing healthcare providers to develop personalized treatment plans for patients.

It is important to test cholesterol levels because high cholesterol can lead to the buildup of plaque in the arteries, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke. Regular testing allows for early detection and management of high cholesterol, helping to prevent cardiovascular complications and maintain overall heart health.

Cholesterol testing is beneficial for individuals of all ages, especially those with risk factors such as a family history of heart disease, obesity, diabetes or a sedentary lifestyle. It is also important for individuals who have already been diagnosed with high cholesterol to monitor their levels and assess the effectiveness of their treatment plan.
