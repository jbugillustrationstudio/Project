What is cholesterol

The word cholesterol is derived from two Greek roots, chole, meaning bile, and stereos, solid, combined with the chemical suffix, -ol, applied in organic chemistry to compounds that have a reactive OH tail also known as an ‘alcohol’.
Chemically, cholesterol is a steroid alcohol, which means it is a chain of hydrocarbons (steroid) with an OH (alcohol) tail attached and it belongs to a group called lipids. Lipids are a family of compounds that includes fats and other important molecules including, cholesterol, triglycerides (TG), phospholipids, and fatty acids .

Most of the cholesterol in your body (about 80 per cent) is not in its pure form. Instead, it is bonded with a fatty acid to create what’s known as a cholesteryl ester, making it more water-soluble. This means there are two types of cholesterol in the body:

  • unesterified (or 'free') cholesterol (found primarily in cell membranes) - unattached cholesterol that is found on the surface of lipoproteins and in cell membranes and can move between the two, and
  • cholesteryl esters (carried in lipoproteins) – cholesterol bonded to fatty acids via a bond between the cholesterol’s hydroxyl group (-OH) and the fatty acid’s carboxyl group (COOH). The result is a cholesteryl ester (used for transport and storage) and a water molecule (a by-product). Cholesteryl esters are hydrophobic (not water-soluble), and more lipid-soluble than free cholesterol, making them ideal for storage and transport in lipoproteins such as low-density lipoproteins (LDL) and high-density lipoproteins (HDL).

Think of it like this: free cholesterol is like cargo on the deck of a ship, easy to access and move, while cholesteryl esters are securely packed below deck, stored and ready for transport3

Transthoracic echocardiogram (TTE) is a non-invasive ultrasound test that provides detailed images of the heart's structure and function. (45 mins)
Electrocardiogram (ECG): This test records the electrical activity of the heart, helping to identify any irregularities in heart rhythm. (5 mins)
Holter monitor is a portable ECG device worn by the patient for 24 to 48 hours to continuously monitor heart rhythm and detect any abnormalities.
Blood tests are conducted to measure cholesterol levels, including total cholesterol, LDL (bad cholesterol), HDL (good cholesterol) and triglycerides, which are essential for assessing cardiovascular health. (?Fasting required)
These examinations are crucial for diagnosing and managing cholesterol-related conditions, allowing healthcare providers to develop personalized treatment plans for patients.

It is important to test cholesterol levels because high cholesterol can lead to the buildup of plaque in the arteries, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke. Regular testing allows for early detection and management of high cholesterol, helping to prevent cardiovascular complications and maintain overall heart health.

Cholesterol testing is beneficial for individuals of all ages, especially those with risk factors such as a family history of heart disease, obesity, diabetes or a sedentary lifestyle. It is also important for individuals who have already been diagnosed with high cholesterol to monitor their levels and assess the effectiveness of their treatment plan.
