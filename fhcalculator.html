For Practitioners

Dutch Lipid Clinic Network Criteria scoring for diagnosis of familial hypercholesterolaemia:

1. Did you have coronary artery disease (ie. heart attack) BEFORE the age of 55 for men, or 60 for woman?
2. Did you had a cerebral or peripheral vascular disease BEFORE the age of 55 for men, or 60 for woman?
3. Do you have a parent, brother or sister that had any premature vascular dissease (heart attack, stroke or peripheral artery disease) BEFORE the age of 55 for men, or 60 for woman?
4. Select your highest known LDL cholesterol level:




5. Do you have a parent, brother or sister that had been told they have LDL level ("bad cholesterol") above 6 mmol/L or above the 95th percentile?
6. If yes, did they have very high LDL ("bad cholesterol") before the age of 18?
7. Have you been told you have a genetic mutation causing familial hypercholesterolemia such as LDLR, APOB or PCSK9 genes or very high LP(a) levels?
8. Do you have any firm, fatty lumps around their tendons (Tendinous xanthomata, i.e. Achilles tendon or hands)? †
9. Do you have a parent, brother or sister that have any firm, fatty lumps around their tendons (Tendinous xanthomata, i.e. Achilles tendon or hands)? †
10. Did you develop a grey or white ring around the coloured part of their eyes (arcus cornealis) before the age of 45? †
11. Do you have a parent, brother or sister that had developed a grey or white ring around the coloured part of their eyes (arcus cornealis) before the age of 45? †
† Image
Achilles tendon (A), the extensor tendons of the hands (B) and/or on the eyelids (palpebral xanthelasma) (C) Arcus cornealis (D)https://www.athero.org.au/fh/health-professionals/how-to-diagnose-fh/#clinicalassessment
